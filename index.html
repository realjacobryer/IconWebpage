Trump V. Teachers

Tax Breaks versus Educator Pay

By Jacob Schermerhorn | Updated October 14, 2020

The New York Times obtained and published findings of Donald Trump's tax information on September 27, 2020. The report revealed federal income taxes, revenues made in foreign countries, an expansive amount of write-offs, and much more. It paints a picture of failing properties and yet millions claimed with lots of debt coming due soon.

Teachers, on the other hand. Have been struggling financially for many years. According to a 2020 study by the American Association of Colleges for Teacher Education, in 2015-2016, 76% of education students took out students loans and that payments on that loan are equivilent to 9% of an average starting teacher's salary. That is above the recommended economist 7% threshold for payments.

This data story will compare findings from the Trump tax story and teachers' financial situation.

The bottom line? One of these groups is much wealthier and has benefited from their status to widen the gap even further.

 

Trump Federal Income Tax in 2017

 

Teacher Federal Income Tax in 2017

 

According to the New York Times report, Donald Trump paid about $750 in U.S. Federal income taxes in 2016 and 2017.

Trump was able to accomplish this by claiming his businesses lost huge amounts of money including a lose of $55 million on a Washington hotel opened in 2016.

This incident has shades of an incident during Richard Nixon's presidency. A 1973 story from The Providence (Rhode Island) Journal revealed that Nixon paid federal income taxes of $792 in 1970 and $873 in 1971 while still in office.

Nixon's salary as president was $200,000 a year. Trump has donated his $400,000 yearly presidental salary to various government agencies but continues to make money from property he owns.

 

According to Bureau of Labor Statistics, teachers, (with an average salary of $57,350) paid $5,403 for Federal income taxes in 2017.

The statistics also show that registered nurses, bus drivers, firefighters and childcare workers, among others, paid more Federal income tax on average than Trump.

For teachers in New York City, that tax number is probably higher. In the 2019-2020 school year, the average salary for a rookie NYC teacher (Bachelor's degree, no prior teaching experience) was $57,845. New teachers with a Master's degree but no teaching experience salary was $65,026. Someone with a Master's degree and eight years experience was $87,510.

Federal Income Tax Refund

 

"Civics For All" Programming cuts

 

The prime subject of the IRS audit is the $72.9 million income tax refund Donald Trump claimed in 2010.

The IRS budget was dramatically cut down during the early 2010s. According to a report by the Taxpayer Advocate Service , the budget was cut by around 20 percent from 2010 to 2019.

Some politicians say the years long conflict with Trump and the IRS is just a symptom of this neglectful trend in funding. Sen. Ron Wyden (Ore), the top Democrat on the Senate Finance Committee, said in a statement that increasing funding for IRS enforcement of abusing tax laws "will be one of my top priorities if Democrats retake the Senate."

 

Facing a $7.4 billion loss in tax revenue, Mayor Bill de Blasio's plan at the outset of the COVID pandemic included saving $3.8 million , by eliminating supplies and materials in the "Civics for All" curriculum and programming.

"Civics for All" was launched in Spring 2018 as a special program for schools wanting to strengthen student civic engagement. These cuts will take place in Fiancial Year 2021 and beyond.

According to nyced.org, the designed cirruculum is intended to teach "the foundations of American government and the democratic process" in order to "build a more engaged, active future citizen and informed voter." Events in the past included Civics Week, a poster contest, and a public speaking event called "Soapbox NYC."

Hairstyling Write Offs

 

Teacher school supplies

 

Donald Trump's wrote off $70,000 worth of hairstyling on his taxes during his time on The Apprentice.

Trump claimed these as a business expense while working on the reality TV show which he hosted from its beginning in 2004 until 2014.

The IRS does have a section for "ordinary and necessary" business expenses for performers. However, the $70,000 figure is particularly large. Speaking to Huffpost , Joey Silvestra, founder of Blackstones and Five Wits salons in New York City, said, doing the math, barring any medical treatment like hair plugs, it would be difficult to get that large of a number.

 

According to 2019 report from Economic Policy Institute, teachers pay $459 out of pocket for school supplies on average.

Teachers in North Dakota spent the least with $327 out of pocket costs while teachers in California spent the most at an average of $666. Teachers in New York spent an average of $480 out of pocket.

It was also found that teachers in high poverty districts spent more than teachers in low poverty districts. It is still unclear whether COVID related supplies will be reimbursable.

Trump Revenue from the Phillipines

 

Health and Technology Education Cuts at NYC Schools

 

Donald Trump's tax records reveal he recieved millions in revenues from foreign countries with authortarian leadership, including $3 million from the Phillipines.

Under a promise of anti-corruption and anti-drug measures, Phillipine President Rodrigo Duterte has continually censored the press, carried out extrajudicial killings, and made homophobic comments. He is also on friendly terms with Trump.

In Turkey, President Recep Tayyip Erdoan has carried out a proxy war in Armenia and suppressed political dissent through intimidation and arrests. Trump once said he was a "big fan of" Erdoan and collected $1 million in revenue from Turkey.

With revenue coming from foreign countries ($73 million in total), some are concerned that Trump could be influenced into favorable trade deals with these countries. Former Deputy Director of the FBI called the possibility of foreign influence on Trump "a Pandora's box."

 

Mayor Bill de Blasio's plan at the outset of the COVID pandemic also included saving $1.8 million , by scaling down Technology curriculum and Health Education Certification programming for teachers.

Health Education is a required part of New York State students curriculum. Health educators are required to teach "behaviors that promote wellness and encourage people to make healthy decisions."

A 2019 Pew Research study found that while more Americans have confidence in science, political divides affect belief in policy decisions. Recent anti-mask rallies, including one in Larimer County , have demonstrated this divide and mistrust of mask wearing which is overwhelmingly supported by health experts.

Consulting Fees for Ivanka Trump

 

Average College Loans for Student Teachers

 

Also revealed in the Trump tax records were $747,622 in consulting fees paid to Donald Trump's daughter, Ivanka.

Ivanka Trump recieved consulting fees paid by the Trump Oganization while simultaneously an employee of the organization. Records show fees for an unnamed consultant for hotel projects in Hawaii and Vancouver, British Columbia. Ivanka's public disclosure forms, filed in 2017, show she recieved an identical amount through a consulting company she co-owned.

This latest revealation could put a damper on Ivanka's efforts to break into the political sphere however. She has long oriented herself as a major member of the Trump political team, by referencing time spent in the oval office and on foreign trips.

 

The average college loan for students studying to become teachers is $28,000

As of 2018, one in five Americans carry student loan debt. The average payment was $200 to $300 a month.

A variety of factors have caused loans to skyrocket including tution increases, which historically have risen at about 3% a year. In the last decade, they have jumped to 26% for private four-year schools and 35% for four-year public schools.
